# Retail Sales Analysis — Excel Dashboard Project

## Overview
This project analyzes 9,800+ rows of retail sales data 
from the Superstore dataset using Microsoft Excel. 
The goal was to identify key business trends across 
regions, product categories, and customer segments 
to support data-driven decision-making.

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel (Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts)

## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle — Superstore Sales Dataset
- Rows: 9,800+
- Columns Used: Order ID, Order Date, City, Region, 
  Category, Sub-Category, Product Name, Segment, Sales

## Analysis Performed
1. Order Volume by City — Identified top and 
   bottom performing cities by number of orders
2. Top 10 Sub-Categories by Sales — Ranked 
   sub-categories by total revenue generated
3. Top 5 Best Selling Products — Identified 
   highest revenue generating individual products
4. Last 30 Days Sub-Category Sales — Analyzed 
   recent sub-category performance trends

## Key Findings
- New York City recorded the highest order volume 
  with 891 orders, while Springfield had the least 
  with 161 orders
- Phones was the top revenue-generating sub-category 
  at $327,782, followed closely by Chairs at $322,822
- Canon imageCLASS 2200 Advanced Copier was the 
  best selling product at $61,599, significantly 
  ahead of all other products
- Phones and Chairs maintained consistent dominance 
  in the last 30 days, confirming sustained demand 
  for these sub-categories
